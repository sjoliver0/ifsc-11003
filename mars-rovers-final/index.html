Exploring Mars — Past, Present, and Future

NASA's Mars rover program has explored the Red Planet with robotic geologists designed to investigate climate, geology, and the potential for past life. This site highlights the Curiosity and Perseverance rovers and the twin MER rovers Spirit & Opportunity.

Curiosity

Primary goal: To determine whether Mars ever had environmental conditions favorable for microbial life.

Launch: November 26, 2011.

Landing: August 6, 2012, at Gale Crater.

Read more →

Perseverance

Primary goal: Search for signs of ancient microbial life and collect samples.

Launch: July 30, 2020.

Landing: February 18, 2021, at Jezero Crater.

Read more →

Spirit & Opportunity

Primary goal: Search for evidence of past water on Mars.

Launches: Spirit — June 10, 2003; Opportunity — July 7, 2003.

Landings: Spirit — Jan 4, 2004; Opportunity — Jan 25, 2004.
